# Lesson 03: Abstract Thinking for Business Ideas

## Objective

Teach the learner how to think beyond the surface of an idea and identify the real business system underneath it.

This is one of the most important lessons in the roadmap.

A great tech salesperson does not only memorize features. They understand the pattern behind the product, the customer, the pain, and the business model.

## What abstract thinking means in sales

Abstract thinking is the ability to move from a specific example to a general principle, then apply that principle to another situation.

Example:

Specific idea:

> A refill reminder system for gas companies.

Abstract principle:

> Some businesses sell products that customers need repeatedly but forget to reorder on time.

Other applications:

- Water delivery
- Pharmacy refills
- School fee reminders
- Salon appointment reminders
- Vehicle service reminders
- Subscription renewal reminders

This is how a salesperson becomes strategic. They stop selling one feature to one customer and start seeing repeatable business patterns.

## The 7-layer idea analysis model

For every idea, analyze it through seven layers.

### Layer 1: Surface idea

What is the obvious idea?

Example:

> A CRM for small businesses.

### Layer 2: User

Who uses it daily?

Example:

- Business owner
- Sales staff
- Cashier
- Marketing officer
- Manager

### Layer 3: Buyer

Who pays for it?

The user and buyer may be different.

Example:

A cashier uses the system, but the business owner pays.

### Layer 4: Pain

What painful problem exists today?

Example:

> The business does not know who its customers are or how to bring them back.

### Layer 5: Existing alternative

What do they use today?

Examples:

- Notebook
- Excel
- WhatsApp contacts
- Memory
- Paper receipts
- Manual calls
- Nothing

### Layer 6: Business value

What measurable outcome can improve?

Examples:

- More repeat purchases
- Fewer missed follow-ups
- Better reporting
- Reduced manual work
- Better staff accountability

### Layer 7: Business model

How can this become revenue?

Examples:

- Monthly subscription
- Setup fee
- Usage fee
- Per branch
- Per staff
- Per campaign
- Enterprise package

## The abstraction ladder

Use this ladder to move from low-level detail to high-level insight.

### Level 1: Feature

> SMS reminders

### Level 2: Workflow

> The business sends customers a message after purchase.

### Level 3: Business process

> Customer follow-up becomes structured instead of random.

### Level 4: Business outcome

> More customers return.

### Level 5: Strategic value

> The business builds a customer retention engine.

A weak salesperson stays at Level 1. A great salesperson sells from Level 4 and Level 5.

## Pattern recognition examples

### Pattern 1: Forgotten repeat purchase

Businesses affected:

- Gas refill companies
- Pharmacies
- Water delivery companies
- Internet service providers
- Beauty salons
- Car service centers

Core pain:

> Customers need the service again, but the business has no system to remind them.

Product value:

> Reminders, segmentation, repeat purchase tracking.

### Pattern 2: Manual record chaos

Businesses affected:

- Schools
- Clinics
- Market associations
- Logistics companies
- Retail shops

Core pain:

> Records are scattered across paper, Excel, WhatsApp, and memory.

Product value:

> Centralized records, reports, user roles, searchable data.

### Pattern 3: No management visibility

Businesses affected:

- Multi-branch businesses
- Associations
- Sales teams
- Field operations

Core pain:

> Leadership does not know what is happening until reports are manually prepared.

Product value:

> Dashboards, branch reports, activity logs, alerts.

### Pattern 4: Trust and control gaps

Businesses affected:

- Financial services
- Payment agents
- Market collections
- Staff-managed businesses

Core pain:

> Owners cannot verify who did what, when, and why.

Product value:

> Audit logs, permissions, staff activity tracking, transaction records.

## How to create product angles

A product angle is the specific way you explain the same product to a specific customer type.

Same product: CRM + messaging + reports.

Gas company angle:

> Refill reminder and repeat sales system.

School angle:

> Parent communication and payment follow-up system.

Market association angle:

> Trader registration, attendance, and revenue visibility system.

Clinic angle:

> Patient follow-up and appointment reminder system.

Retail angle:

> Customer loyalty and repeat purchase tracking system.
