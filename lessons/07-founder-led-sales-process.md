# Lesson 07: Founder-led Sales Process

## Objective

You will learn how to run a basic but serious sales process from prospecting to close.

Founder-led sales is not random talking. It is a repeatable operating system.

## Sales pipeline stages

Use this simple pipeline:

```text
Lead -> Qualified Lead -> Discovery -> Demo -> Proposal -> Negotiation -> Closed Won / Closed Lost -> Onboarding
```

## Stage 1: Lead

A lead is a possible customer.

Sources:

- Personal network
- Referrals
- Business directories
- LinkedIn
- WhatsApp groups
- Events
- Existing business relationships
- Walk-in visits
- Partnerships

## Stage 2: Qualified lead

A qualified lead has:

- A real business problem
- Ability to pay
- Decision-maker access
- Some urgency
- Fit with the product

Qualification questions:

- Is this the right type of customer?
- Do they have the problem?
- Can they pay?
- Who decides?
- Why now?

## Stage 3: Discovery

This is where you ask questions and understand the business.

Do not demo too early.

## Stage 4: Demo

Show only what connects to the pain discovered.

## Stage 5: Proposal

A proposal should summarize:

- The problem
- The recommended solution
- The scope
- Pricing
- Timeline
- Next steps

## Stage 6: Negotiation

The goal is not to win an argument. The goal is to find a structure that works without destroying value.

## Stage 7: Closed won or closed lost

Closed lost is not failure if you learn why.

Track loss reasons:

- Price
- Timing
- No budget
- Not decision maker
- Missing feature
- Trust issue
- Weak urgency

## Stage 8: Onboarding

A sale is not complete until the customer is using the product.

## Sales operating rhythm

Every week, track:

- New leads added
- Discovery calls done
- Demos completed
- Proposals sent
- Follow-ups made
- Deals closed
- Objections heard
- Product feedback collected

## Prospecting message template

```text
Hello [Name], I noticed [business context].

We help [type of business] solve [specific pain] by [simple outcome].

I would like to understand how you currently handle [process]. Would you be open to a short conversation this week?
```

## Follow-up rule

Most deals are lost because salespeople do not follow up.

Follow up:

- Same day after meeting
- 2 days later
- 1 week later
- After agreed deadline

Each follow-up should add value, not just say “checking in.”

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Build a lead list

Create a list of 50 potential customers.

For each:

- Business name
- Industry
- Contact person
- Phone/email
- Reason they may need the product
- Status

### Exercise 2: Pipeline setup

Create a simple pipeline in a spreadsheet or CRM tool.

Stages:

- Lead
- Qualified
- Discovery
- Demo
- Proposal
- Negotiation
- Closed Won
- Closed Lost

### Exercise 3: Weekly sales review

At the end of the week, answer:

- What did we learn?
- Which segment responded best?
- What objection repeated?
- Which pitch worked?
- What must change in the product or message?

## Deliverable

Create:

`sales-pipeline.md`

Include your pipeline stages, lead criteria, weekly metrics, and follow-up rules.
