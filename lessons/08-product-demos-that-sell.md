# Lesson 08: Product Demos That Sell

## Objective

You will learn how to run demos that connect the product to customer pain.

A demo is not a product tour. A demo is a business conversation supported by the product.

## The biggest demo mistake

Bad demo:

> This is the dashboard. This is the customer page. This is the settings page. This is the campaign page.

This makes the customer work too hard to understand the value.

Good demo:

> You mentioned that customers do not return regularly. Let me show you how you can record them, group them, and send reminders.

## Demo principle

Show less. Make it matter more.

## Demo structure

### 1. Recap the pain

Start with what the customer told you.

> From our conversation, the main issue is that customer follow-up is manual and inconsistent.

### 2. Confirm before showing

> Is that still the main problem you want to solve first?

### 3. Show the relevant workflow

Only show what connects to that pain.

### 4. Explain the business outcome

> This means your team can know who has not returned and reach them before they forget you.

### 5. Ask for reaction

> How close is this to how your team works today?

### 6. Discuss implementation

> What would need to happen for your staff to start using this?

### 7. Close on next step

> The next step would be a small pilot with your team and a clear success metric.

## Demo scripts by pain

### Pain: Customers do not return

Demo:

- Add customer
- Record sale
- Segment customer
- Send reminder
- Show report

Business story:

> This creates a repeat sales engine.

### Pain: Poor reporting

Demo:

- Record transaction
- Show dashboard
- Filter by date
- Export report

Business story:

> This gives management visibility without waiting for manual reports.

### Pain: Staff accountability

Demo:

- Staff login
- Role permissions
- Activity record
- Audit trail

Business story:

> This helps the owner know who did what.

### Pain: Manual messaging

Demo:

- Customer category
- Message template
- Campaign send
- Campaign report

Business story:

> This helps the business communicate faster and more consistently.

## Demo language to use

Use:

- “This helps you...”
- “The business benefit is...”
- “Your team would use this when...”
- “This reduces...”
- “This gives you visibility into...”
- “This supports the workflow you described...”

Avoid:

- “As you can see...”
- “This feature is very powerful...”
- “We have many modules...”
- “The backend does...”
- “It is AI-powered” unless the value is clear

## Demo checklist

Before demo:

- Know the buyer
- Know the user
- Know the pain
- Know the current process
- Know the desired outcome
- Know the likely objection

During demo:

- Start with pain
- Show relevant workflow
- Pause often
- Ask questions
- Avoid unnecessary features
- Connect to value

After demo:

- Send recap
- Confirm next step
- Share proposal if needed
- Record objections
- Update pipeline

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Create demo paths

Create 3 demo paths:

- Customer retention demo
- Reporting demo
- Staff control demo

### Exercise 2: Mock demo

Run a mock demo with someone pretending to be a skeptical business owner.

### Exercise 3: Demo reflection

After each demo, answer:

- What problem did I demo against?
- Did I show too much?
- What confused the customer?
- What made them interested?
- What next step did I secure?
