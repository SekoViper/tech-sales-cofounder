# Lesson 02: Tech Product Foundations

## Objective

Teach the learner enough technology language to sell confidently without pretending to be a developer.

The goal is business-level understanding, not coding ability.

## The rule

A commercial co-founder must understand technology well enough to:

- Explain the product simply
- Avoid overpromising
- Understand customer concerns
- Communicate with developers
- Translate business needs into product requirements
- Translate product features into customer value

## What a tech product really is

A tech product is a repeatable system that helps a user complete an important job.

It normally has:

- Users
- Data
- Workflows
- Rules
- Reports
- Notifications
- Integrations
- Permissions
- Business logic

A customer does not care about the code. They care about whether the product helps them do work better.

## Key concepts

### SaaS

Software as a Service. The customer pays to use software, usually monthly or yearly.

Business meaning:

> Instead of buying software once, customers pay for continuous access, updates, support, and value.

Sales question:

> What ongoing value does the customer receive every month?

### CRM

Customer Relationship Management.

Business meaning:

> A system for recording customers, understanding their history, and managing follow-up.

Sales question:

> How do you currently know who your best customers are?

### POS

Point of Sale.

Business meaning:

> A system used to record sales transactions.

Sales question:

> How do you currently record sales, and how do you know the records are accurate?

### API

Application Programming Interface.

Business meaning:

> A secure way for two systems to communicate.

Example:

A payment system can send a confirmation to a business platform after a customer pays.

Sales question:

> Which other systems does your business need this product to connect with?

### Database

A structured place where data is stored.

Business meaning:

> The memory of the business system.

Sales question:

> What important information do you currently lose, duplicate, or fail to track?

### Dashboard

A visual summary of important business information.

Business meaning:

> A dashboard helps managers see what is happening without waiting for manual reports.

Sales question:

> What numbers do you wish you could see every morning?

### Automation

Software performing repeated tasks with less manual effort.

Business meaning:

> Automation reduces delay, forgetfulness, and repetitive work.

Sales question:

> What task does your team repeat every day or every week?

### Integration

Connecting one system to another.

Business meaning:

> Integration reduces double entry and makes systems work together.

Sales question:

> Where does your team currently enter the same information twice?

### User roles and permissions

Different users see and do different things.

Business meaning:

> Permissions protect business control.

Sales question:

> Who should be allowed to see sales, edit records, approve actions, or view reports?

## How to explain technical ideas simply

Do not say:

> We have a multi-tenant cloud-based CRM with role-based access control and messaging automation.

Say:

> Each business gets its own secure account. Staff can only access what they are allowed to use. The business can record customers, send messages, and view reports from one place.

## Business translation table

| Technical concept | Business translation |
|---|---|
| Database | Business memory |
| Dashboard | Management view |
| API | System handshake |
| Automation | Work done without manual chasing |
| User roles | Control over who can do what |
| Cloud | Accessible online without installing locally |
| Backup | Protection against data loss |
| Audit log | Record of who did what |
| Integration | Systems working together |

## Abstract thinking drill

Take any technical feature and ask:

1. What work does this feature help the user complete?
2. What mistake does it prevent?
3. What time does it save?
4. What money can it protect or create?
5. What decision does it improve?

Example:

Feature:

> Staff login

Abstract value:

> Staff login is not just authentication. It creates accountability. The business owner can know who recorded a sale, who served a customer, and who changed data.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Explain like a business owner

Explain these terms in one sentence each:

- SaaS
- CRM
- API
- Dashboard
- Automation
- Integration
- User role
- Database

### Exercise 2: Product map

Choose one product and map:

- Main users
- Data collected
- Workflows supported
- Reports generated
- Notifications sent
- Integrations needed
- Business value created

### Exercise 3: Developer-to-customer translation

Translate this sentence:

> The platform supports role-based access control, multi-currency reporting, and asynchronous notification callbacks.

Into simple customer language.
