# Lesson 04: Customer Discovery

## Objective

You will learn how to speak with customers to discover real pain before selling.

Customer discovery is the foundation of founder-led sales. If you cannot ask good questions, you will build weak pitches and chase the wrong customers.

## The rule

Do not ask customers to validate your idea. Study their behavior.

Bad questions:

- Do you like this idea?
- Would you use this?
- Would you pay for this?
- Is this useful?

Better questions:

- How do you solve this today?
- When was the last time this problem happened?
- What did it cost you?
- Who was involved?
- What did you try before?
- What happens if this continues?

## Why people give false validation

People often say nice things because:

- They want to encourage you
- They do not want to offend you
- They are imagining an ideal future version
- They are not the real buyer
- They do not feel the pain strongly enough
- They have no budget

Your job is not to collect compliments. Your job is to collect evidence.

## Discovery conversation structure

### Step 1: Context

Ask about the business.

- What type of business do you run?
- How many customers do you serve monthly?
- How many staff do you have?
- How do customers usually buy from you?

### Step 2: Current process

Understand the workflow.

- How do you currently record customers?
- How do you follow up with customers?
- How do you track repeat purchases?
- How do you prepare reports?

### Step 3: Pain

Look for frustration.

- What is difficult about this process?
- What mistakes happen often?
- What takes too much time?
- Where do you lose visibility?

### Step 4: Cost

Quantify the pain.

- How much time does this take weekly?
- How much money could this problem be costing?
- How often does the problem happen?
- How many customers might you be losing?

### Step 5: Alternatives

Find the real competition.

- What do you use today?
- Why did you choose that method?
- What have you tried before?
- Why did it not work?

### Step 6: Urgency

Find out if they care now.

- Is this a problem you want to solve soon?
- What would make you prioritize it?
- Who else must approve the decision?
- What would stop you from adopting a solution?

## The customer discovery mindset

Do not lead the witness.

Instead of:

> Would a CRM help you manage customers better?

Ask:

> How do you currently manage customer information?

Instead of:

> Would SMS reminders help customers return?

Ask:

> What do you do when a customer has not returned for a long time?

Instead of:

> Would you pay $100 monthly?

Ask:

> Have you paid for any software or system to solve this before?

## Signals of real pain

Strong signals:

- They already tried solving it.
- They spend money or time on a workaround.
- The problem affects revenue.
- The owner complains emotionally.
- Staff make repeated mistakes.
- Reports are delayed or unreliable.
- They ask about price or implementation.
- They introduce you to another decision maker.

Weak signals:

- They say “interesting.”
- They say “nice idea.”
- They say “maybe later.”
- They want many features but no commitment.
- They cannot explain when the problem last happened.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Build a discovery script

Create a 20-question discovery script for one target industry.

### Exercise 2: Conduct interviews

Interview at least 10 prospects this week.

Document:

- Business type
- Role of person interviewed
- Current process
- Main pain
- Existing workaround
- Urgency level
- Possible next step

### Exercise 3: Pain evidence log

For each conversation, write one real quote from the customer.

Example:

> “Sometimes customers forget to refill, and by the time we call them, they have already bought from another supplier.”

## Deliverable

Create:

`customer-discovery-notes.md`

It should include notes from at least 10 customer conversations.
