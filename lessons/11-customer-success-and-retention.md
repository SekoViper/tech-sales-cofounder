# Lesson 11: Customer Success and Retention

## Objective

Teach the learner that closing a customer is not the finish line. It is the beginning of value delivery.

A commercial co-founder must help customers succeed after the sale.

## Why customer success matters

Software businesses grow when customers stay, use the product, and expand.

If customers buy but do not use the product, they will churn.

Churn means:

- Lost revenue
- Weak referrals
- Damaged trust
- More pressure to sell new deals

## The success equation

```text
Customer expectation + Product usage + Measurable value + Support = Retention
```

## Onboarding checklist

A customer should not be left alone after payment.

Basic onboarding steps:

- Confirm business goals
- Confirm success metrics
- Create users
- Import data
- Configure settings
- Train staff
- Run first workflow
- Review first report
- Schedule follow-up

## First 30 days success plan

### Day 1

- Welcome call
- Confirm goals
- Assign responsible person
- Create account

### Day 3

- Upload/import data
- Train first users
- Complete first workflow

### Day 7

- Check usage
- Solve blockers
- Confirm staff adoption

### Day 14

- Review first results
- Send first campaign or report

### Day 30

- Business review
- Compare success metric
- Agree next step

## Usage metrics

Track:

- Logins
- Customer records added
- Sales recorded
- Campaigns sent
- Reports viewed
- Staff active
- Support requests
- Feature usage

## Business value metrics

Track:

- Repeat customers
- Customer engagement
- Revenue influenced
- Time saved
- Errors reduced
- Reports produced
- Staff accountability improved

## Monthly business review

A monthly review should answer:

- What was used?
- What value was created?
- What problem remains?
- What should improve next month?
- Is the customer ready to renew or expand?

## Expansion opportunities

Once a customer succeeds, identify:

- More users
- More branches
- More campaigns
- More modules
- Integrations
- Custom reports
- Annual contract
