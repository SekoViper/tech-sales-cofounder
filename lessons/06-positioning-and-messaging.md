# Lesson 06: Positioning and Messaging

## Objective

Teach the learner how to explain a tech product so customers immediately understand why it matters.

Positioning answers:

> What is this product, who is it for, and why should they care now?

Messaging turns that positioning into words customers understand.

## Bad positioning

> We are a CRM platform with analytics, messaging, customer segmentation, inventory, sales tracking, and dashboards.

This is feature-heavy and unclear.

## Strong positioning

> We help small businesses turn customer records into repeat sales by tracking customers, sending targeted reminders, and showing business performance in one dashboard.

This is clearer because it explains:

- Who it is for
- What it helps them do
- Why it matters

## Positioning formula

```text
For [target customer]
who struggle with [pain],
our product is a [category]
that helps them [business outcome],
unlike [current alternative],
we [key difference].
```

Example:

> For gas retailers who struggle to bring customers back for refills, Yinava is a customer retention and messaging platform that helps them track customers, predict refill opportunities, and send reminders, unlike manual phone calls or WhatsApp lists, it gives structured records and reports.

## Product category matters

Customers understand products through categories.

You may call the same product different things depending on the market:

- Customer retention system
- CRM platform
- Sales and customer follow-up system
- Loyalty and messaging platform
- Business reporting dashboard
- Merchant operating system

Choose the category that makes sense to the buyer.

## Message hierarchy

### Level 1: One-line message

> Bring customers back with automated follow-up and simple business reporting.

### Level 2: Short pitch

> Our platform helps businesses record customers, track purchases, send targeted reminders, and see performance reports so they can increase repeat sales and improve control.

### Level 3: Full pitch

> Many businesses serve customers once and lose track of them. They depend on paper, Excel, WhatsApp, or staff memory. This makes follow-up weak and reporting slow. Our platform gives them a structured way to record customers, group them, send messages, track sales, and understand business activity.

## Industry-specific messaging

### Gas company

> Know when customers are due for refill and remind them before they buy elsewhere.

### School

> Organize parent records, registration follow-ups, payment reminders, and communication from one system.

### Clinic

> Improve patient follow-up, appointment reminders, and service history tracking.

### Market association

> Track traders, staff attendance, revenue collection, and market activity across locations.

### Retail shop

> Record customers, reward loyalty, and bring buyers back with targeted offers.

## Messaging mistakes

Avoid:

- Too many features
- Too much jargon
- Talking before understanding the customer
- Claiming unrealistic results
- Sounding like every other software company
- Positioning for everyone

Strong messaging is specific.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Write five one-liners

Write five one-line messages for the same product, each for a different customer segment.

### Exercise 2: Feature detox

Take a feature-heavy paragraph and rewrite it without using more than two feature names.

### Exercise 3: Category test

List five possible categories for the product. Choose the one customers understand fastest.
