# Lesson 12: Co-founder Execution System

## Objective

Turn the learner from a student into an operating commercial co-founder.

A co-founder must produce measurable outcomes every week.

## The commercial co-founder scorecard

Track weekly:

- New leads added
- Discovery conversations completed
- Demos booked
- Demos completed
- Proposals sent
- Follow-ups completed
- Deals won
- Deals lost
- Revenue closed
- Product feedback collected
- Customer success check-ins

## Weekly operating rhythm

### Monday: Pipeline planning

- Review open leads
- Choose priority prospects
- Set meeting targets
- Prepare outreach messages

### Tuesday to Thursday: Sales execution

- Prospect
- Run discovery calls
- Demo product
- Follow up
- Send proposals

### Friday: Learning review

Ask:

- What did customers say repeatedly?
- Which pain is strongest?
- Which objection is common?
- Which segment is easiest to sell?
- Which feature matters most?
- What should the product team know?

## Founder feedback loop

The commercial co-founder must feed the product team with useful insight.

Bad feedback:

> Customers want more features.

Good feedback:

> Three gas retailers asked for refill reminders because they currently call customers manually. The repeated pain is not messaging itself, but not knowing who is due for refill. We should prioritize purchase history and reminder scheduling before advanced loyalty.

## Decision discipline

A co-founder should know when to say:

- This is a real opportunity
- This is a distraction
- This customer is not a fit
- This feature request is too custom
- This segment has urgent pain
- This price is too low for the support required

## Monthly review

Every month, prepare a commercial report:

```text
Leads generated:
Discovery calls:
Demos:
Proposals:
Closed won:
Closed lost:
Revenue:
Top 5 objections:
Top 5 customer pains:
Product feedback:
Next month focus:
```

## Personal growth system

The learner should build weekly habits:

- Read one sales/business resource
- Listen to one founder interview
- Practice one demo
- Review one lost deal
- Improve one pitch
- Speak to at least five prospects

## Final co-founder standard

A commercial co-founder must be able to answer:

- Who are we selling to?
- What pain are we solving?
- Why now?
- Why us?
- What proof do we have?
- What is the price?
- What is the sales process?
- What is blocking deals?
- What is the product team learning from the market?

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Build weekly scorecard

Create a weekly dashboard for sales activity.

### Exercise 2: Lost deal review

Analyze one lost deal:

- What happened?
- What was the real objection?
- Was the customer qualified?
- Was the value clear?
- What should improve?

### Exercise 3: Product feedback memo

Write a memo to the product team summarizing what customers are asking for and what should be prioritized.

## Deliverable

Create:

`cofounder-commercial-operating-system.md`

Include weekly scorecard, monthly review, feedback loop, and personal improvement plan.
