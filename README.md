# Tech Sales Co-founder Roadmap

A 12-week roadmap for you if you come from an accounting background and want to become a commercial co-founder who can sell technology products, think abstractly about business problems, and turn ideas into revenue.

This curriculum is designed for you if you do **not** need to become a software engineer, but must become strong enough to:

- Understand technology products at a business level.
- Identify painful business problems worth solving.
- Translate abstract ideas into customer value.
- Sell software products with confidence.
- Run customer discovery, demos, proposals, negotiations, and onboarding.
- Think like a co-founder, not just a salesperson.
- Use accounting discipline to discuss pricing, ROI, risk, control, reporting, and business outcomes.

## Core belief

Do not position yourself as “an accountant learning tech.”

Position yourself as:

> I help businesses use technology to improve revenue, customer retention, reporting, and operational control.

The goal is to become a **commercial co-founder with financial discipline**.

## Who this is for

This roadmap is for:

- Accountants transitioning into tech business leadership.
- Non-technical co-founders who need to sell software.
- Early startup team members responsible for sales and partnerships.
- Founders building B2B SaaS, CRM, fintech, merchant tools, operations software, or AI-enabled business products.

## How to use this repo

Study one lesson per week. Each lesson contains:

- Learning objective
- Founder mindset
- Detailed explanation
- Practical frameworks
- Exercises
- Sales drills
- Deliverables
- Recommended resources

Do not only read. You must talk to real customers, write proposals, practice demos, and document feedback.

## 12-week roadmap

| Week | Lesson | Main outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Founder Mindset and Commercial Identity | Think like a business problem solver, not only an accountant |
| 2 | Tech Product Foundations | Explain SaaS, CRM, APIs, dashboards, automation, and integrations simply |
| 3 | Abstract Thinking for Business Ideas | Turn vague ideas into problems, systems, users, value, and business models |
| 4 | Customer Discovery | Ask questions that reveal real pain instead of polite opinions |
| 5 | Problem, Pain, and Value Mapping | Connect customer pain to measurable business value |
| 6 | Positioning and Messaging | Explain why the product matters and who should care |
| 7 | Founder-led Sales Process | Build a repeatable sales pipeline from prospecting to close |
| 8 | Product Demos That Sell | Demo based on customer pain, not feature tours |
| 9 | Objection Handling and Negotiation | Handle price, trust, adoption, timing, and competition objections |
| 10 | Pricing, ROI, and Commercial Models | Use accounting strength to defend pricing and structure deals |
| 11 | Customer Success and Retention | Onboard customers and prove ongoing value |
| 12 | Co-founder Execution System | Build weekly operating habits, metrics, and feedback loops |

## Learning rule

For every idea, force yourself to answer:

1. Who has the problem?
2. What painful job are they trying to complete?
3. What happens if the problem is not solved?
4. What does the customer currently use instead?
5. Why should they pay now?
6. What result must the product create?
7. How do we prove value in numbers?

## Completion standard

You have completed this roadmap only when you can:

- Explain the product in plain business language.
- Interview at least 20 prospects.
- Run at least 5 product demos.
- Create at least 3 industry-specific sales messages.
- Write at least 2 proposals.
- Build a pricing model.
- Handle common objections.
- Create an onboarding checklist.
- Produce a customer success review.
- Present a final go-to-market plan.

## Important warning

A co-founder title means nothing without measurable contribution.

Track yourself by outcomes:

- Number of customer conversations
- Number of qualified leads
- Number of demos booked
- Number of proposals sent
- Number of deals closed
- Revenue generated
- Customer usage after onboarding
- Product feedback collected

If those numbers are not improving, you are studying sales, not becoming a salesperson.
